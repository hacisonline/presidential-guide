01 The app

The building, in your hands

The Presidential runs like a fine hotel — a staffed lobby, a 24×7 control room, automated stacker parking with valet, a rooftop with a pool, sky lounge and pickleball court, and services on call. Tesseract Home is its digital front desk: one app where you and your team handle everything the building does, without a phone call, a WhatsApp to the manager, or a paper register.

One model

Every screen, one building

Visitors, parking, bookings, bills and requests are windows into one living model of the building — a digital twin, not separate systems stapled together.

Auditable

Everything leaves a trail

Every approval, pass, payment and decision records who did it and when. Trust is shown, not claimed.

Private

Your data stays with the building

The concierge answers from the building's own records. Nothing sold, nothing tracked — the intelligence is built to live in-house.

Two experiences, one app. On a phone it's a thumb-first column with a tab bar. On a desktop browser it becomes a full application with a top navigation and wide canvas. They switch automatically and carry the same features — nothing is phone-only or desktop-only.

02 Reception

The concierge is the front door

The app opens on Reception — a living, breathing mark that listens, thinks and speaks. Ask it about the building the way you'd ask a concierge at a great hotel: by voice or by typing.

Ask it

What Reception knows

Amenities and timings, house rules, guest parking, visitor passes, directions, services — the building's community knowledge. Try "pool timings" or "how do visitor passes work".

Honesty

What it won't do

It never invents an answer. If the building's records don't cover your question, it says so — and offers to pass it to the team as a tracked request, number and all.

Voice

Speak or type

Tap the microphone to talk; spoken questions get spoken replies. Typing always works, everywhere.

Alerts

Critical notices greet you

A lift shutdown, a water interruption — anything critical surfaces on Reception and Home until you've read it, then steps aside everywhere at once.

Swipe left to reach Home and the modules; Reception stays one tap away. And the intelligence behind it is engineered around a simple promise — the building owns its brain.

From invitation to the doors opening

Three house courtesies

Smoke-free save the terrace corner · deliveries via the service lift · quiet hours 10 pm to 7 am. Acknowledge, and you're in.

Live · your app

The visitor journey — auditable at every hop: who cleared whom, for which unit, when. Check-out frees the guest bay automatically.

Walk-ins

You decide, quickly

Someone arrives unannounced? Security registers them and you get an approve/decline card with a countdown. Silent past the window? Security may decide — and that override is logged as an override.

Blocklist

Not at your door

Add a name or number your unit won't receive; pass creation refuses instantly and the gate checks again on arrival. Your visitor log is yours alone.

06 Living with it

The rooftop, the boardroom, the court

Eight amenities — meeting rooms, the rooftop sky lounge, the pool, pickleball, the café's private dining and The Terrace event space. Book in seconds; the house enforces its own rules, so double-bookings simply cannot happen.

Pick the space and the slot

Every card shows capacity, hours, notice required and the rate if chargeable. Choose date, start, duration, guests.

The rules engine answers

A taken slot refuses plainly — "That slot is taken." Notice windows, opening hours, durations, blackout dates and capacity are enforced for you and everyone else alike.

Confirmed — or sent for approval

Most spaces confirm instantly; the sky lounge, private dining and The Terrace go to management first and come back decided.

Charges ride to your bill

Chargeable spaces price by the hour, pro-rata — 90 minutes of the ₹2,500/hour boardroom is ₹3,750 — and land on your monthly invoice once the booking completes. Cancel in good time and the charge clears; a late cancel keeps it, and says so.

07 Living with it

Proper tax documents, quiet payments

Every invoice is a real GST document — the building's GSTIN and yours, HSN/SAC per line, the CGST/SGST split, sequential immutable numbering. No screenshots of spreadsheets.

Invoices

CAM, utilities, amenities

Monthly CAM on your chargeable area, amenity charges swept in automatically, utilities and one-offs — with due dates, and "overdue" always computed live.

Pay

Tap, confirm, receipt

Pay from the invoice; a numbered receipt issues instantly, cross-referenced to the payment. The amount always comes from the invoice itself — never from a screen that could lie.

Statement

Your running ledger

Opening balance, every invoice and payment, closing balance — provably tied to what's outstanding. Offline payments recorded by the office appear here too.

08 Living with it

Raise it, track it, close it yourself

Anything the building should fix — AC, electrical, plumbing, housekeeping, the lift — goes in with photos if you like. Reception's escalations land in the same queue, so nothing you ask ever falls between systems.

Every request carries a service promise, shown up front:

12 The building team

The gate desk

Built for standing use in the lobby: big targets, one clear action per card, an audit trail behind every movement. The 24×7 control room's daily companion.

Verify

Scan or search

QR scan, or the one-time code, phone or name among today's expected. Check-in enforces the code, refuses outside the visit window, and re-checks the blocklist at the door — a denial is logged.

Walk-ins

Route to the host

Register the walk-in; the unit decides with a countdown. After the timeout the desk may decide — recorded as an override, with the note to match.

Material

The material register

Clear or reject requested passes (a rejection needs a reason the unit will read), then log the movement when it happens. Every state carries who and when.

The day

Inside now & the log

Everyone currently inside on a pass, one-tap check-out that frees their guest bay, expected arrivals, the full day log.

13 The building team

The valet & facilities desks

Two lean queues, each with one clear action per card and the discipline held by the system, not by memory.

Valet

Start → Ready → Handed over

The queue in arrival order with car, request type and asked-for time. No skipped states, no two active requests on one car, the ready time stamped for the record — and the guest-pool count always in view.

Facilities

Pick up → start → resolve

Pick up unassigned work, never a colleague's. Resolving takes a note because the unit reads it. Late resolution marks the promise breached on the record; reopened jobs return with full history and the clock running.

14 The building team

The management portal

The building at a glance, then every module in depth. The dashboard reads the same living model the app does: who's inside, today's bookings, open requests and promise breaches, collections with the past-due slice, parking occupancy, recent notices.

Notices

Compose & broadcast

Type, priority, audience of everyone / floors / units — validated against the real building. Schedule ahead, set an expiry, preview the exact card users will see, and watch read counts.

Requests

The board & the promise

Every request by status with assign/reassign, plus promise performance: open, overdue now, via Reception, settled in 30 days, % kept within promise.

Amenities

Approvals & the report

Approve or decline pending bookings — approval re-checks the calendar. The 30-day report reads bookings, hours, occupancy and revenue per amenity.

Billing

The CAM run & collections

Preview the month (rate × chargeable area, finished amenity charges swept in, skips named) — then issue with sequential immutable numbering. Record offline payments; watch collections by unit and aging that tie out to the paisa.

Users & units

The registry

Invite users by phone against a unit, set relations and primaries, manage parking allotments, read the directory by floor.

Administration

Accounts, guarded

Add anyone, rename, change roles, disable — with an activity log in plain sentences and guardrails: nobody edits themselves, the last administrator is protected, history is never erased.

The segregation model underneath

Surfaces

Role-scoped by construction

Each role gets its own surface; a screen you shouldn't have doesn't exist for you, and pages refuse to fetch data for the wrong role.

Isolation

Unit isolation at the core

Every query is filtered to the asking user's own units at the data layer — deny by default. One rule, one place, inherited by everything above it, the AI included.

Audit

An actor on every write

Approvals, decisions and payments carry their people. Intelligence added on top inherits a fully attributed history.

Today, honestly: the shipping concierge answers from the building's community knowledge only — retrieval over the building's own records, so it can cite its sources and admit what it doesn't know. Voice can be simple speech to start. The step to a local language model on the in-house appliance changes the brain, not the rules: personal answers will only ever be read through the same identity gate you just traced.
16 Good to know

Small print, kept honest

Prototype

What's simulated today

Sign-in uses a demo persona switcher (phone + OTP arrives with the production phase), payments settle as a simulation, and pass delivery is shown rather than sent. Each swaps for the live service without changing anything else in this guide.

Demo data

The prototype resets

The demo runs on representative seeded data that resets on restart — deliberate for evaluation. Production brings the building's own database, on the building's own server.

Etiquette

The three courtesies

Smoke-free save the designated terrace corner; deliveries and material via the service lift; quiet hours in shared areas, 10:00 pm – 7:00 am.

Help

When something's off

Ask Reception first — and if it can't resolve it, let it raise the request for you. A tracked number and a service promise, in under a minute.
